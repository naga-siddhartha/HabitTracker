Privacy Policy — Habits

Last updated: March 2025

Habits (“the app”) is a habit-tracking app for Apple platforms. This privacy policy describes what data the app collects, how it is used, and your choices. It is provided to comply with App Store Review Guidelines (including Guideline 5.1.1) and to give you clear, accurate information.

1. Data we collect, how we collect it, and how we use it

Data stored only on your device (we do not receive this data):

  • Habit data: Habit names, descriptions, icons, colors, and frequency settings. We collect this when you create or edit habits. It is used only to display and manage your habits in the app.
  • Completion and history: Dates you complete or skip habits and any skip reason. We collect this when you mark a habit done or skipped. It is used for your calendar, statistics, and streaks.
  • Reminders: Reminder times and names you set. We collect this when you configure reminders. It is used only to send you local notifications on your device.
  • Streaks: Current and longest streak per habit. This is derived from your completion history and used only to show streak information in the app.
  • App preferences: For example, appearance (light/dark/system) and whether notifications are enabled. We collect this when you change settings. It is used only to apply your preferences in the app.

This data is stored locally using Apple’s SwiftData framework. No account or login is required. The app does not collect your name, email, or other personally identifying information for habit functionality.

2. Advertising (third-party)

The app may display ads provided by Google (Google Mobile Ads / AdMob). Google may collect and use data (such as device identifiers and ad interaction data) to serve and measure ads, in accordance with Google’s Privacy Policy. Any user data shared with or processed by this advertising network is subject to the same or equal protection of user data as described in this privacy policy and required by applicable guidelines. You can control ad personalization and other settings via your device and Google’s ad settings.

3. Data retention and deletion

  • Data on your device: Habit data, history, and preferences remain on your device until you delete them or remove the app.
  • How to revoke consent and request deletion: You can delete all habit-related data at any time by using “Reset All Data” in the app’s Settings. Uninstalling the app removes all of its stored data from your device. We do not retain copies of your habit data on our servers because we do not receive or store it.

4. Third parties and data protection

Aside from the advertising provider noted above (Google), we do not share your habit data with third parties. Any third party that receives or processes data in connection with the app (such as the advertising network) is required to provide the same or equal protection of user data as stated in this privacy policy and as required by the Apple Developer Program and App Store Review Guidelines.

5. No transmission of your habit data

Your habit names, completion history, reminders, and preferences are never sent to our servers or to the internet. The app does not make network requests for your habit data. Only the advertising SDK may communicate with its own servers for ad serving, as described in the Advertising section above.

6. Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top will be revised when we do. Continued use of the app after changes means you accept the updated policy.

7. Contact

For questions about this privacy policy or the app, use the “Contact & Support” link in the app’s Settings (which opens your email client), or the support information listed on the App Store page for Habits.
